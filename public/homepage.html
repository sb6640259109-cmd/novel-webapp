The King's Awakening
by Ellen Voss · 📚 Chapter 124/126
80% complete · Resume from paragraph 118, Bookmark 43
— FROM THE CHRONICLE —

When the bells of Aeris rang at dawn, every carven stone set beneath the palace stones began to answer back.

Kael stood alone in the shattered observatory, one hand braced against the frost-laced window while the city below woke in ripples of gold and ash. The throne hall was three levels beneath him, yet he somehow heard the court gathering like a storm around the name he had spent half his life trying to outrun.

A second bell rolled across the towers. It passed, echoing through the glass, through the iron inlaid at his wrist, through the old scar at the center of his chest. The relic hidden in the chamber floor answered with a low pulse of blue light, as if the kingdom itself had finally decided to recognise its heir.

Behind him, Lyaera closed the door without a sound. She did not bring the letter he had asked for. Instead, she placed a folded dispatch on the nearest table and watched him with the calm expression of someone who had already accounted for the cost of every possible future.

"The sentinel gate opened before sunrise," she said. "Not for soldiers. For nobles. They are sending nobles deep into our courtyard before ceremonies even begin." Kael cut a breath that was almost a laugh. He really had not always loved a ceremony, especially when he arrived dressed as a disaster.

He unfolded the dispatch. The seal belonged to House Vandermaal — a blade quartered with a torch, deeply scorched at the edges with a line written in a hand he knew too well. It was unlike a single line written in a hand he knew too well. If he knew the country better, the misconnection will open again.

For a moment the room went quiet enough for him to hear the relic beneath the floor breathe. The words had followed him from exile to the capital, but none of them frightened him as much as the possibility that the message was true.

Kael folded the note and tucked it into his coat. "Then we stop pretending this is a courtroom," he said, turning from the stairwell. "It is a stage. And I intend to arrive before my enemies remember which door they left unchained."
